Satta, also known as "Satta Matka," is a form of gambling that originated in India. The term "satta" means "betting" in Hindi. It involves participants placing bets on the opening and closing rates of cotton traded on the New York Cotton Exchange. Over time, the practice expanded to include betting on various other factors, such as the opening and closing rates of other commodities, stock markets, and even random numbers.
Satta is primarily a game of chance, with participants selecting a number or combination of numbers and placing bets on them. The results are typically announced through various mediums, such as newspapers, websites, or word of mouth. The game has gained popularity over the years and has become a significant source of income for some individuals. However, it's important to note that gambling, including satta, is illegal in many jurisdictions due to its associated risks and potential for fraud.

In the Matka game, participants place bets on numbers ranging from 0 to 9. These bets are placed on various combinations, such as single numbers, pairs, or triplets. The winning numbers are determined by a process that involves drawing three numbers from a matka (pot) at random. For example, if the drawn numbers are 3, 6, and 9, the winning bet might be based on the sum of these numbers, which in this case would be 18. Matka gained popularity in the 1960s and 1970s when it was widely played in the bustling textile mills of Mumbai. However, with the passage of time, gambling laws became stricter, and the activity was banned in India. Despite the ban, Matka still continues to be played in some parts of the country, often in an underground and illegal manner.

The origins of Satta Matka can be attributed to a man named Kalyanji Bhagat, who was a farmer from Gujarat. He started the practice of offering bets on the opening and closing rates of cotton traded on the New York Cotton Exchange. Initially, the bets were placed on the variation of cotton rates, which were transmitted from the New York Cotton Exchange to Mumbai via teleprinters.
With time, the game expanded beyond cotton rates to include betting on various other factors, such as the opening and closing rates of other commodities, stock markets, and even random numbers. Rattan Khatri, another prominent figure, played a significant role in shaping the game by introducing additional features and new variations.
The game gained immense popularity among the working class and businessmen in Mumbai. It provided them with an exciting and potentially lucrative form of entertainment. The Matka business started booming, and numerous Matka centres or outlets sprung up across the city, attracting a large number of participants.

Single: In Satta Matka, a single is a bet on a single digit between 0 and 9. For example, betting on the number 5 is considered a single.

Jodi: Jodi refers to a pair of two-digit numbers between 00 and 99. Participants can place bets on the combination of these two numbers.

Patti: Patti refers to a three-digit number result in Satta Matka. It is derived from the sum of the three individual digits. For example, if the sum of the three digits is 18, the Patti number would be 8.

Open Result/Closing Result: Open result is the first digit drawn from the Matka, while the closing result is the second digit drawn. Participants can place bets on these individual numbers.

Berij: Berij refers to the result obtained by subtracting the closing result from the open result. For example, if the open result is 7 and the closing result is 3, the Berij would be 4.
